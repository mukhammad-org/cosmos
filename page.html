What do we know about planets?

Planets are celestial bodies that orbit stars, with our solar system having eight: four inner terrestrial (rocky) planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) and four outer giant planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune).
Planets form from a collapsing interstellar cloud through accretion, and they are categorized by composition and features like solid surfaces, moons, or ring systems.
